Research Experience

SEP 2025 — MAY 2026
MSc Dissertation Researcher — TRIDENT Safety System
UK National Robotarium · Heriot-Watt University · Supervisor: Prof. Oliver Lemon
Designed and implemented a Triple-Layer Defence-in-Depth (DiD) safety architecture for LLM-driven domestic robots, guarding the full perception → planning → execution stack.

Layer 1 — Perception Guard: CLIP ViT-L/14 Mahalanobis OOD detection; reduced adversarial ASR from 58.3% → 3.2% across FGSM, PGD, patch and noise variants without attack-specific tuning.

Layer 2 — Semantic Guard: NuSMV CTL formal verification of 47 domestic safety rules; 30/32 properties proved over a 6,144-state model — to our knowledge one of the first such verifications for LLM-driven domestic agents.

Layer 3 — Reference Monitor: Robust adversarial defences against 11 Bi-Modal Adversarial Prompt (BAP) variants. Real-time: rule path 0.14 ms · CLIP path 7.8 ms — both within 500 ms action-cycle requirement.

Novel Contributions

01
First Formal CTL Verification of LLM-Driven Domestic Robots
Applied NuSMV model checking to runtime safety verification — to our knowledge one of the first such verifications, proving 30/32 CTL properties over a 6,144-state model.
02
Pareto-Dominant Safety Architecture
TRIDENT simultaneously achieves best safety rate (99.3%), precision (1.000), F1 (0.9952), and adversarial robustness — outperforming GPT-4o, Constitutional AI, and all rule-based baselines across every metric.
03
Zero False Positives on 450-Task Benchmark
Precision = 1.000 maintained throughout evaluation, demonstrating that robust safety need not sacrifice task usability or throughput.
04
94.5% Adversarial Attack Reduction
CLIP ViT-L/14 Mahalanobis OOD detector reduced ASR from 58.3% to 3.2% across 11 Bi-Modal Adversarial Prompt variants without attack-specific fine-tuning — a significant generalisation result.
05
Real-Time Safety Within Domestic Robot Constraints
Rule-based path 0.14 ms (>3,500× margin) and full VLM path 7.8 ms — both well within the 500 ms domestic action-cycle requirement, enabling practical deployment.
06
Novel BAP Benchmark Construction
Constructed and systematically evaluated a 450-task benchmark spanning 11 Bi-Modal Adversarial Prompt categories — FGSM, PGD, patch, and noise variants for multimodal robot safety evaluation.
07
Human Trust & Robot Anthropomorphism Study
Empirically demonstrated that robotic response speed is a key lever in managing anthropomorphisation of AI systems — IEEE ISTAS 2025 — contributing evidence-based HRI design guidelines.
08
End-to-End Humanoid Robot Campus Deployment
Designed, built, and deployed ARRK — achieving 98.12% emotion recognition in a live environment with multimodal speech, vision, and NLP integration. Published IEEE ICCR 2024.
